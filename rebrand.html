Origin

Founded in 2008 by Nour Al Hassan, Tarjama was built in Amman and Dubai with a network of women working from home — remote before remote was a category. The vision held: from a regional translator to MENA's largest language and content company. $15M Series A in 2025 from Global Ventures and Endeavor Catalyst made the next chapter inevitable.

Act II · Three honest observations

So why now.

Three things became impossible to ignore. None of them are about taste. All of them are about the position the brand was already in — whether we acknowledged it or not.

01

The world went AI-native. The old surface didn't.

In 2008, "translation company" was the right shelf to live on. In 2026, that shelf is a footnote. Tarjama already builds fine-tuned LLMs, agentic platforms, Arabic-first AI infrastructure. The work is AI-native. The wordmark wasn't. A logo from another decade can't carry the position the work earned.

02

The market never stopped saying "Tarjama".

The "&" was supposed to do a job: signal that the company is more than translation. Seventeen years of equity didn't follow it. Clients still write "Tarjama". Press still writes "Tarjama". Search still finds "Tarjama". The ampersand added visual complexity to solve a problem the market wasn't actually having. We listened — and removed it.

03

Next to Arabic.AI, the old wordmark looked like a different era.

Arabic.AI is a sibling brand, not a competitor — and on every co-branded deck, signage, and collateral, the two were visibly from different decades. Same family, different DNA. The new Tarjama wordmark shares the modern, bold typographic register Arabic.AI is built on, so when they appear together they finally read as one company speaking with one voice.
